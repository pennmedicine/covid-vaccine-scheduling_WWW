Penn Medicine Employee COVID-19 Third Dose and Booster Shot Vaccine Scheduling

Penn Medicine is currently providing third doses and boosters to the following employee groups:

  1. Employees who have severely weakened or compromised immune systems (severely immunocompromised) can receive a third dose of Pfizer or Moderna.
    Who is considered severely immunocompromised?
  2. Employees who are not immunocompromised and received two doses of Pfizer at least six months ago can receive a booster shot of Pfizer.

Employees who are not severely immunocompromised and received Moderna are not eligible for a booster shot at this time, consistent with CDC guidance.

Unlike last winter, vaccines are now widely available. If you are eligible, you have the option to receive a third dose or a booster shot at Penn Medicine or from your local pharmacy.

If you are eligible to receive a third dose (severely immunocompromised) or a booster shot (at least six months since second dose of Pfizer-BioNTech), click the “Get Started” button below to login using your Penn Medicine network username and password.

Who is considered severely compromised?

  • Currently receiving active treatment with chemotherapy or daily radiation therapy for cancer (such as lung cancer, breast cancer and/or cancer of the blood (leukemia or lymphoma)).
  • Recipient of an organ transplant and taking immunosuppressive therapy to prevent organ rejection, or are currently being treated for graft versus host disease after transplant.
  • Undergone CAR-T-cell or had a stem cell transplant within the last 2 years and currently taking immunosuppression therapy.
  • Diagnosed with moderate or severe primary immunodeficiency (e.g., DiGeorge syndrome, Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome, Severe Combined Primary Immunodeficiency, Chronic Granulomatous Disease, Common Variable Immunodeficiency, Congenital Neutropenia Syndrome, X-Linked Lymphoproliferative Disease, X-Linked Agammaglobulinemia, and others)
  • Advanced or untreated HIV infection with a CD4 cell count less than 200 cells
  • Currently taking one or more of the following medications in doses at or above what is listed:
    • Prednisone 20 mg. daily or more for 2 or more weeks
    • Dexamethasone 3 mg. daily
    • Methylprednisolone 16 mg daily
    • Hydrocortisone 80mg daily
    • Methotrexate (e.g., Otresup, Rasuvor, Rheumatrex or Trexall) 0.4 mg/kg/week or more
    • Azathioprine (e.g., Azasan or Imuran) 3 mg/kg/day or more
    • 6-mercaptopurine (e.g., Purinethol or Purixan) 1.5 mg/kg/day or more
    • Rituximab (e.g. Rituxan, Truxima, Ruxience, or Riabni)
    • Nataluzimab (e.g. Tysabri)
    • Alemtuzumab (e.g. Lemtrada)
    • Eculizumab (e.g., Soliris)
