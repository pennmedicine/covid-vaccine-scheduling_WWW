Penn Medicine Employee COVID-19 Vaccine Booster Scheduling

Penn Medicine is currently providing the new bivalent COVID-19 Vaccine Booster to employees.

  • Employees who received two doses of Pfizer or Moderna at least two months ago or one dose of the Johnson & Johnson vaccine at least two months ago can receive the new booster shot.
  • Employees who received any COVID vaccine booster at least two months ago can receive the new booster shot.

If you are immunocompromised, please talk with your doctor about whether you should receive additional vaccine doses.

Vaccines are now widely available. If you are eligible, you have the option to receive the new booster shot at Penn Medicine or from your local pharmacy.

Before coming to a booster appointment at Penn Medicine, be sure to read the Preparing for your Appointment information below. It contains a link to print out your bar-coded vaccine form which you will need to bring to your appointment.

Preparing for your appointment

Appointments can be scheduled either during or outside of your work shift. Vaccine recipients will not be required to stay for a post-dose 15-minute observation, unless there is a history of severe allergic reactions, in which a 30-minute observation time is required. UPHS paid employees will be paid for the time required for the appointment, including any observation period. Before scheduling your appointment during a work shift, please coordinate with your manager to minimize impact on patient care and operations.

Before coming to your scheduled appointment:

  • Print out your employee barcoded Moderna/Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine booster consent form to bring to the appointment
  • Review the EUA Fact Sheets for either the Moderna or Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 Booster Vaccine.
  • Bring your CDC COVID-19 Immunization Card that documents your primary vaccine series.

By scheduling and appearing for a vaccine booster appointment, you are consenting to receive care at Penn Medicine. The following documents are relevant to this care:

  • Penn Medicine General Consent to Care forms
  • Penn Medicine HIPAA form
  • Penn Medicine COVID Safety FAQs

If you have any questions about the Penn Medicine General Consent to Care form or are not ready to agree, please let the Penn Medicine staff know when you arrive for your appointment. If you have additional questions, please check out these frequently asked questions.
