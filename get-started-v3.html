Penn Medicine Employee COVID-19 Vaccine Booster Scheduling

Penn Medicine is currently providing COVID-19 Vaccine Boosters to the following employee groups:

  1. First Booster: Employees who received two doses of Pfizer or Moderna at least five months ago or one dose of the Johnson & Johnson vaccine at least two months ago can receive their first booster shot.
  2. Second Booster: Employees who are 50 or older and received their initial booster shot at least 4 months ago can receive a second booster shot.

Vaccines are now widely available. If you are eligible, you have the option to receive a booster shot at Penn Medicine or from your local pharmacy.

Before coming to a booster appointment at Penn Medicine, be sure to read the Preparing for your Appointment information below. It contains a link to print out your bar-coded vaccine form which you will need to bring to your appointment.

Preparing for your appointment

Appointments can be scheduled either during or outside of your work shift. Vaccine recipients will be required to stay for a 15-minute observation (or 30 minutes if there is a history of severe allergic reactions) after receiving the booster dose of the vaccine. UPHS paid employees will be paid for the time required for the appointment, including the observation period. Before scheduling your appointment during a work shift, please coordinate with your manager to minimize impact on patient care operations.

Before coming to your scheduled appointment:

  • Print out your employee barcoded Moderna/Pfizer-BioNTech or Janssen (Johnsons & Johnson) vaccine form to bring to the appointment
  • Review the Penn Medicine COVID-19 Vaccine mRNA (Pfizer and Moderna) Fact Sheet or the Janssen (Johnsons & Johnson) Fact Sheet.
  • Review the EUA Fact Sheets for Moderna, Pfizer-BioNTech or Janssen (Johnsons & Johnson) COVID-19 Vaccines
  • Bring your CDC COVID-19 Immunization Card that documents your primary vaccine series

By scheduling and appearing for a vaccine booster appointment, you are consenting to receive care at Penn Medicine. The following documents are relevant to this care:

  • Penn Medicine General Consent to Care forms
  • Penn Medicine HIPAA form
  • Penn Medicine COVID Safety FAQs

If you have any questions about the Penn Medicine General Consent to Care form or are not ready to agree, please let the Penn Medicine staff know when you arrive for your appointment. If you have additional questions, please check out these frequently asked questions.
